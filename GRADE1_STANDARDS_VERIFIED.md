## 1. Verification findings — read this first

The locked-at-76 draft was built from a Grade-2 mental model. TAC verification revealed Grade 1's actual structure differs in three load-bearing ways. None of these are typos — they are the rule text disagreeing with the draft.

### 1.1 Reading sub-strand numbering is shifted by one (Grade 1 has a strand Grade 2 doesn't)

In §110.4 (Grade 2), comprehension sits at strand (b)(6); response at (b)(7); literary elements at (b)(8); literary characteristics at (b)(9); informational characteristics at (b)(10); author's craft at (b)(11).

In §110.3 (Grade 1), comprehension is at (b)(6) too — but Grade 1 also has (b)(4) **fluency** and (b)(5) **self-sustained reading** as their own strands. The strand counts work out the same, but the user-facing dotted codes shift:

| Topic | Draft used | TAC says (Grade 1) |
|---|---|---|
| Comprehension skills | `1.5.X` | `1.6.X` |
| Response skills | `1.6.X` | `1.7.X` |
| Multi-genre literary elements | `1.7.X` | `1.8.X` |
| Literary text characteristics (children's lit, poetry, drama, folktales) | `1.8.X` | (folded into) `1.9.X` |
| Informational text characteristics | `1.9.X` | (folded into) `1.9.D` (single sub-letter with Roman sub-items) |
| Author's craft | `1.10.X` | `1.10.X` ✓ |

The biggest consequence: **Grade 1 has no separate strand for "literary text characteristics" vs "informational text characteristics."** They're one strand — `(b)(9) genres` — with sub-letters covering children's literature (A), poetry (B), drama (C), informational (D with three Roman sub-items: i/ii/iii), persuasive (E), multimodal/digital (F).

The draft's `1.8.A through 1.8.D` and `1.9.A through 1.9.D` collapse into TAC's single `1.9.A through 1.9.F`.

### 1.2 Three TEKS sub-letters were missed by the draft

- **`1.3.A`** — "use a resource such as a picture dictionary or digital resource to find words." Excluded by the draft. Testable as MCQ.
- **`1.9.E`** — "recognize characteristics of persuasive text…" Missed by draft. Testable as MCQ.
- **`1.9.F`** — "recognize characteristics of multimodal and digital texts." Missed by draft. Borderline-testable; included for completeness.

### 1.3 Vocabulary affixes in `1.3.C` are wrong in the draft

The draft says affixes `un-, re-, -ly, -er`. TAC §110.3(b)(3)(C) says **`-s, -ed, -ing`**. (The draft's set is from Grade 2 §110.4(b)(3)(C); Grade 1 introduces inflectional endings, not derivational morphology.)

### 1.4 `1.10.B` content is misassigned in the draft

The draft says 1.10.B is "Discuss how author uses words to create images." Per TAC, **`1.10.B` is "discuss how the use of text structure contributes to the author's purpose."** The "words to create images" content is `1.10.D`.

### 1.5 Language conventions: TAC has 10 sub-letters under `(11)(D)`, not 14

The draft's `1.11.D.i through 1.11.D.xiv` over-decomposes. TAC §110.3(b)(11)(D) lists exactly:

```
(i)   complete sentences with subject-verb agreement
(ii)  past and present verb tense          ← "future" is NOT Grade 1
(iii) singular, plural, common, and proper nouns
(iv)  adjectives, including articles
(v)   adverbs that convey time
(vi)  prepositions
(vii) pronouns: subjective, objective, possessive cases  ← not "personal/possessive"
(viii) capitalization for sentence-start and "I"
(ix)  end punctuation: declarative, exclamatory, interrogative
(x)   spelling: orthographic patterns + high-frequency words
```

The draft's separate spelling rows for CVC, CVCe, and high-frequency words don't exist in TAC — they're one sub-letter (x). The draft's "sentence completeness" (xiii) and "sentence types" (xiv) don't exist as standalone Grade 1 items either.

### 1.6 Math `1.2.B` description is missing "hundreds"

TAC says: "compose and decompose numbers up to 120 in more than one way as so many hundreds, so many tens, and so many ones." Draft omitted "hundreds." Trivial fix.

### 1.7 What I didn't change

Math structurally matches the draft — same standards in/out, just exact-text descriptions. `1.5.A` (recite numbers oral), `1.3.E` (explain strategies oral), `1.6.C` (create 2D figures process) stay excluded as the draft had them. Personal financial literacy `1.9.A–D` dropped per the locked decision.
