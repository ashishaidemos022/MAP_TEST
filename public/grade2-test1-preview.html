Money — Count and Word Problems

82.5.A Count money
How much money is shown?
A) $2.60
B) $2.50
C) $2.75
D) $2.35
$1 + $1 + 25¢ + 25¢ + 10¢ = $2.60
92.5.A Count money — bills only
How much money is shown?
A) $3.00
B) $7.00
C) $11.00
D) $15.00
$5 + $1 + $1 = $7
102.5.B Money word problem
Priya saved 1 ten-dollar bill and 4 dimes. She bought a notebook for $7.80. How much does she have left?
A) $2.20
B) $2.60
C) $3.20
D) $1.60
$10.40 − $7.80 = $2.60
112.5.B Money — combine and compare
Aarav has $13.45. Mia has $8.70. How much more money does Aarav have?
A) $4.25
B) $4.75
C) $22.15
D) $5.25
$13.45 − $8.70 = $4.75
122.5.B Money — three people total
Noah has $14.20. Zoe has $9.85. Leo has $6.30. They want to buy a $35 game. Do they have enough?
A) No — they need 65¢ more
B) Yes — they have $1.35 left
C) No — they need $5 more
D) Yes — they have exactly enough
$14.20 + $9.85 + $6.30 = $30.35. $35 − $30.35 = $4.65 short.
132.5.B Two-step money
Ethan had $9. He earned $4 raking leaves, then spent $7.50 on a book. How much does he have now?
A) $5.00
B) $5.50
C) $6.00
D) $1.50
$9 + $4 = $13. $13 − $7.50 = $5.50.

Fractions

372.3.D Compare fractions
Which fraction is the greatest?
A) 1/2
B) 1/3
C) 1/4
D) All equal
Same whole, fewer pieces = bigger pieces. Halves > thirds > quarters.
382.3.D Compare like-denominator
Which fraction is less? Same-size pizza, different slices shaded.
A) 5/8
B) 3/8
C) Equal
D) Cannot tell
Same size pieces (eighths). Fewer shaded = less.
392.3.A Fraction of a set
What fraction of the shapes are circles?
A) 3/6

C) 3/3
D) 6/6
3 circles out of 6 total shapes.
